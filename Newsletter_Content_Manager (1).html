How to Use

Everything you need to get the most out of Newsletter Content Manager

🚀 Getting started

  1. Set your newsletter name — click the name in the top-left sidebar to edit it to your own newsletter name.
  2. Track your audience — go to the Audience tab and log your follower/subscriber counts per platform (LinkedIn, Substack, Medium, etc.). The total auto-calculates and shows in the sidebar. Save a snapshot monthly to track growth over time.
  3. Add your first article — go to Article Tracker, paste your article link, fill in the stats, and hit Save.
  4. Build your idea backlog — go to Idea Backlog, add ideas and score them. The app ranks them automatically.
  5. Plan ahead — use Publishing Calendar to schedule upcoming posts with date, time, and platform.

📊 Article Tracker — how to use

  1. Paste your article link — the platform auto-detects based on the URL.
  2. Upload a cover image — save your article's cover image to your computer, then click the upload box. It stores the image inside the app.
  3. Log stats after publishing — come back 48–72 hours after publishing to log your views, open rate, clicks, and likes.
  4. Fill in "What worked / didn't" — this is the most valuable field. One sentence is enough. It powers the What's Working tab over time.
💡 Tip: Log stats consistently at the same interval (e.g. always at 48 hours) so your data is comparable across articles.

📅 Publishing Calendar — how to use

  1. Add a scheduled post — enter the title, pick a platform, date, time, and status.
  2. Use it as a planner — add posts weeks in advance so you always know what's coming up.
  3. Status options: Idea (just an inkling), Draft (writing in progress), Scheduled (ready to go).
  4. Past posts automatically move to the "Past scheduled posts" section once the date has passed.
💡 Tip: Aim to have at least 2 weeks of content planned ahead in the calendar at all times.

💾 Saving your data — important

Your data is saved in your browser's local storage. This means it persists between sessions on the same browser and device — but will be lost if you clear your browser cache or switch devices.

To protect your data: use the Export backup button in the sidebar regularly. This downloads a .json file with all your articles, ideas, and calendar entries.

To restore your data on a new device or after a browser reset, click Import backup and select your .json file.

Recommended habit: Export a backup once a month, or after any major session where you've added a lot of data.

💡 Name your backup files with dates: backup_jan_2026.json
